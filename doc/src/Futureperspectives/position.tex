\begin{frame}{Purpose and caveat}
\begin{itemize}
\item This is a non-exhaustive scouting list, not a ranking.
\item The emphasis is on researchers who appear relevant for future searches in
\begin{itemize}
\item quantum computing and computational many-body physics,
\item quantum information and algorithms,
\item quantum control and simulation,
\item quantum sensing and metrology.
\end{itemize}
\item ``Young'' is interpreted broadly as early-career to relatively young mid-career.
\item Several names below are already independent group leaders or assistant professors and may therefore represent
\begin{itemize}
\item realistic hires,
\item ambitious targets, or
\item benchmark profiles for a search committee or people to ask advice from.
\end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

%------------------------------------------------
\begin{frame}{What we may like to see}
\begin{itemize}
\item Strong publication record in top venues in quantum information, quantum optics, AMO physics, or condensed matter
\item Clear independence from PhD and postdoctoral supervisors
\item Ability to bridge theory, algorithms, and experiment when relevant
\item Visibility through ERC-style funding, invited talks, prizes, or leadership roles
\item Synergy with local strengths in
\begin{itemize}
\item quantum computing,
\item quantum metrology and sensing,
\item machine learning,
\item computational many-body physics.
\end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}


\begin{frame}{Possible Evaluation Axes}
\begin{itemize}
\item Scientific independence and originality
\item Breadth versus specialization
\item Ability to connect to teaching in physics, mathematics, and computer science
\item Synergy with local infrastructure and collaborations
\item Potential for ERC Starting/Consolidator-level success
\item Potential for building bridges between
\begin{itemize}
\item quantum computing,
\item quantum metrology,
\item AI/ML,
\item computational many-body physics.
\end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
