Nuclear Theory at FRIB: Educational and Research opportunities

Morten Hjorth-Jensen

Department of Physics and Astronomy and FRIB/NSCL Laboratory, Michigan State University, USA

MSU-JYFL mini-workshop June 10












Nuclear theory at FRIB https://nscl.msu.edu/researchers/theory/index.html

  1. Approximately \( \sim 60-70 \) researchers and staff, faculty (11), graduate students and undergraduate students (\( \sim 40-50 \)) and post-doctoral fellows and FRIB-TA fellows (\( \sim 10 \)).
  2. Research spans from fundamental symmetries and quantum chromodynamics, via effective field theories to nuclei and infinite matter (cold or hot and dense nuclear matter)
  3. FRIB Theory Alliance (FRIB-TA https://fribtheoryalliance.org/)











Research topics and potential overlaps

  • Lattice QCD
  • Effective field theories on the lattice
  • Reaction theory for low-energy nuclear physics
  • Relativistic heavy-ion physics
  • Nuclear Astrophysics
  • Few and many-body theories
    1. Full configuration interaction theory (shell model) and effective operators
    2. Coupled Cluster theory, In-medium Similarity Renormalization Group, Density Functional theory, Monte Carlo methods and more
  • Machine Learning and Bayesian Inference applied to few and many-body problems and classification of experimental events
  • Quantum computing and quantum information theories applied to nuclear physics problems
  • and more











Educational overlaps and possibilities

  • Played a significant role in establishing and running the Nuclear TALENT initiative https://fribtheoryalliance.org/TALENT/
  • Offers a vast spectrum of nuclear theory courses in house (from nuclear structure to LQCD)
  • Active in guiding research undergraduate students and organizing schools and workshops for students
  • Excellent opportunity for student exchanges at all levels, from undergraduates to Master of Science and PhD students.
  • Full exchange of credits for semesters abroad with established agreement (for exchange students tuition is paid at home institution)
  • Many more mobility possibilities
